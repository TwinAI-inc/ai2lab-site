CONFIDENCE INTERVAL

CI for One Mean (σ Known)

FORMULA
$$\bar{X} \pm z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$
KEY z VALUES
90% CI: $z_{0.05} = 1.645$
95% CI: $z_{0.025} = 1.960$
99% CI: $z_{0.005} = 2.576$
CONDITIONS
$\sigma$ is known
Normal pop. or $n \ge 30$
Random sample

QUESTION

Battery Life: How Long Will It Last?

A manufacturer tests 36 batteries. The sample mean lifetime is 48.5 hours. From historical data, the population standard deviation is known: σ = 5.2 hours.

Task: Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true mean battery life.
5 / 18Prof. Mohammad DehghaniNortheastern University
SOLUTION

Battery Life: 4-Step Answer

STEP 1 — DEFINE
Let $\mu \triangleq$ true mean battery life (hours)
Goal: 95% CI for $\mu$
STEP 2 — IDENTIFY
Z-Interval
$\sigma$ known, $n=36 \ge 30$ (CLT), random sample
STEP 3 — COMPUTE
$$\bar{x} \pm z_{0.025} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} = 48.5 \pm 1.96 \cdot \frac{5.2}{\sqrt{36}} = 48.5 \pm 1.699$$
$$\boxed{CI:\;(46.80,\; 50.20)\text{ hours}}$$
STEP 4 — INTERPRET
"We are 95% confident the true mean battery life is between 46.80 and 50.20 hours."

CONFIDENCE INTERVAL

CI for One Variance

FORMULA
$$\left(\frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{\alpha/2}},\; \frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{1-\alpha/2}}\right)$$
Take $\sqrt{\phantom{x}}$ for a CI on $\sigma$
CONDITIONS
Normal population required
$\text{df} = n - 1$, random sample
Sensitive to non-normality
The $\chi^2$ distribution is asymmetric — so the CI is not centered on $s^2$.

THE FRAMEWORK

Hypothesis Testing

Assume the status quo ($H_0$) is true until data provides strong enough evidence against it — like "innocent until proven guilty."

HYPOTHESES
$H_0$: Null — no effect, status quo
$H_1$: Alternative — what we suspect
ERROR TYPES
Type I (α)
Reject true $H_0$
False alarm
Type II (β)
Keep false $H_0$
Missed signal
LEFT-TAILED
$H_1: \mu < \mu_0$
TWO-TAILED
$H_1: \mu \ne \mu_0$
RIGHT-TAILED
$H_1: \mu > \mu_0$

KEY CONCEPT

What is a P-value?

The probability of seeing data this extreme (or more) if the null were true.

VISUAL INTUITION
$\text{p-value} = P(\text{Z} \geq z_{\text{obs}} \mid H_0 \text{ true})$
DECISION RULE
<
p < α  →  Reject H₀
data is too surprising under H₀
≥
p ≥ α  →  Fail to Reject
data is consistent with H₀
⚠ COMMON PITFALLS
  • p-value is NOT the probability H₀ is true
  • p-value is NOT the probability the result happened by chance
  • p > α does NOT prove H₀ — it means "insufficient evidence"
  • A small p does NOT imply practical importance

QUESTION

Coffee Machine: Under-Dispensing?

A coffee shop claims their machine dispenses 12 oz per cup. A health inspector tests 25 cups and finds $\bar{x} = 11.7$ oz with $s = 0.8$ oz.

At $\alpha = 0.05$: Is there evidence the machine dispenses less than claimed?
12 / 18Prof. Mohammad DehghaniNortheastern University
SOLUTION

Coffee Machine: 4-Step Answer

STEP 1 — HYPOTHESES
$$H_0: \mu = 12 \qquad H_1: \mu < 12$$
Left-tailed (claim: under-dispenses)
STEP 2 — TEST STATISTIC
$\sigma$ unknown, $n=25$ → t-test
$$t = \frac{11.7 - 12}{0.8 / \sqrt{25}} = \frac{-0.3}{0.16} = \boxed{-1.875}$$
STEP 3 — DECISION
Critical Value
$t_{\text{crit}} = -1.711$ (df=24)
$-1.875 < -1.711$ → Reject $H_0$
P-value
$p\text{-value} \approx 0.0365$
$0.0365 < 0.05$ → Reject $H_0$
STEP 4 — INTERPRET
"At the 5% significance level, there is sufficient evidence that the machine dispenses less than 12 oz per cup."

TWO SIDES OF ONE COIN

Confidence Interval vs Hypothesis Test

CONFIDENCE INTERVAL
Answers: "What range of values is plausible for $\mu$?"
Battery example: The true mean life is somewhere between 46.8 and 50.2 hours (95% confident).
HYPOTHESIS TEST
Answers: "Is there evidence against a specific claim?"
Coffee example: At α=0.05, there IS evidence the machine dispenses less than 12 oz (reject $H_0$).

What You Now Know

CI ↔ HT CONNECTION
If $\mu_0$ falls outside the $(1{-}\alpha)$ CI, you reject $H_0$ at level $\alpha$. Two sides of the same coin.
CI FORMULAS
Mean ($\sigma$ known): $\bar{x} \pm z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \sigma/\sqrt{n}$
Mean ($\sigma$ unknown): $\bar{x} \pm t_{\alpha/2} \cdot s/\sqrt{n}$
Variance: $\chi^2$ interval, df=$n{-}1$
TEST STATISTICS
Z-test: $z = (\bar{x}-\mu_0)/(\sigma/\sqrt{n})$
t-test: $t = (\bar{x}-\mu_0)/(s/\sqrt{n})$
Decision: p-value vs $\alpha$
📚
FUN FACT
Fisher's choice of $\alpha = 0.05$ was "convenient" — yet it became the gold standard for a century of science.
